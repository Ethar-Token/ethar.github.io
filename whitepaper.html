Executive summary

ETHAR is a mobile-first token distribution platform that mints virtual token balances via short, repeatable mining sessions activated by ad views. The model is designed to onboard mainstream users quickly while funding platform operations and preparing for a secure, KYC-backed migration to Ethereum mainnet.

1. Vision & rationale

ETHAR makes token ownership easy: simple ad‑activated mobile mining sessions, a transparent referral program, and short session durations that encourage repeat engagement. Ads fund platform costs while referrals and boosts drive organic growth and retention.

2. Tokenomics

Key parameters:

  • Max supply: 100,000,000 ETHAR (fixed)
  • Initial mining pool: 80,000,000 ETHAR
  • Team allocation: 10,000,000 ETHAR (vested)
  • Treasury / liquidity / marketing: 10,000,000 ETHAR
  • Registration bonus: 5 ETHAR to both referrer and referee

Distribution and vesting details are configurable but recommended to include long‑form vesting for team allocation and gradual treasury use for liquidity.

3. Mining mechanics

Mining is off-chain in the app (virtual balances) and follows simple rules:

  • Activation: Watch an ad to completion → 24‑hour mining session starts.
  • Base rate: 0.2 ETHAR / hour.
  • Referral bonus: +0.05 ETHAR / hour per active referral (an active referral is a referee who is currently in a mining session).
  • Ad boosts: Each ad boost adds +0.05 ETHAR / hour for 6 hours. Max 2 active boosts.
  • Session behaviour: Mining continues if the app is backgrounded; it stops on logout.

Rate formula (per hour):

rate = 0.2 + (active_referrals × 0.05) + (active_ad_boosts × 0.05)

Tokens mined are deducted from the mining pool in real time. Pool balances are shown in human friendly form (e.g. 80.0M, 79.6M, 21.3K).

4. Anti-dump & lockups

To protect value at migration we recommend:

  1. Default migration lockup (e.g., 50% locked for 3–12 months) applied to mined balances at mainnet conversion.
  2. Lockup incentives: bonuses for choosing longer or larger lockups.
  3. Team vesting: 24 months with a 6-month cliff.
  4. Gradual liquidity provisioning from treasury to reduce price shocks.

5. Revenue & sustainability

Ads fund platform operations. Suggested splits:

  • 60% operations & development
  • 25% liquidity & buybacks
  • 15% marketing & community

Ad-driven sessions are intentionally lightweight — the user value (24h mining) is higher than the ad interruption cost, encouraging retention.

6. KYC & mainnet migration

We will roll out KYC before March 20, 2026 to comply with AML requirements and to prepare for on-chain migration. KYC enables secure token transfer to user-supplied wallet addresses, participation in listings, and regulatory compliance.

Migration plan (high level):

  1. KYC roll-out and completion
  2. Smart contract audit and security review
  3. Snapshot & migration with lockups applied
  4. Liquidity provisioning & exchange listing coordination

7. Roadmap

  • Now: User acquisition, product polish.
  • Q4 2025: KYC tooling, audit prep.
  • Q1 2026: KYC roll-out, audit of migration contracts.
  • Mar 20, 2026: Mining scheduled end (app balances ready for migration).
  • Q2 2026: Target mainnet migration and initial exchange listings.

8. Appendix — example math & notes

Example: User with base 0.2/hr + 3 active referrals + 2 boosts = 0.2 + 0.15 + 0.10 = 0.45/hr → 24h = 10.8 ETHAR.

Technical recommendations: ERC‑20 on Ethereum or L2 (Arbitrum/Optimism) for lower gas; smart contracts audited; treasury in multisig (Gnosis Safe).
